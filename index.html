My Top 10 Games Ever

The definitive ranking. No arguments.

#1 Elden Ring

A masterpiece of open-world design from FromSoftware. Elden Ring takes everything that made Dark Souls legendary and expands it into a vast, breathtaking world co-created with George R.R. Martin. Every corner hides a secret. Every boss is a war.

Metacritic 96/100

"An incredible achievement in game design that sets a new standard for the genre."

— IGN
#2 GTA V

Over a decade later and GTA V still holds up as one of the greatest open-world games ever made. Three protagonists, a sprawling Los Santos, and an online mode that became a game in its own right. Rockstar's magnum opus.

Metacritic 97/100

"A stunning achievement that raises the bar for open-world games once again."

— Eurogamer
#3 Red Dead Redemption 2

Rockstar's other masterpiece. A slow-burn epic about loyalty, loss and the dying of the American frontier. Arthur Morgan is one of gaming's greatest protagonists. The storytelling here rivals anything in cinema.

Metacritic 97/100

"A landmark moment for interactive storytelling and open-world design."

— The Guardian
#4 Dota 2

The most complex and rewarding competitive game ever made. Thousands of hours in and you're still learning. Dota 2 demands everything from you and gives back in equal measure. The International remains esports' greatest spectacle.

Metacritic 90/100

"The deepest, most rewarding competitive experience in gaming."

— PC Gamer
#5 Fortnite

Love it or hate it, Fortnite changed gaming forever. The battle royale that crossed over into mainstream culture, collaborating with everyone from Marvel to Nike. Still evolving, still relevant, still ruthlessly fun.

Metacritic 81/100

"A cultural phenomenon that redefined what a live service game could be."

— Polygon
#6 The Last of Us

Naughty Dog's post-apocalyptic masterpiece redefined narrative gaming. The relationship between Joel and Ellie is one of the most emotionally powerful in all of fiction. The HBO adaptation proves just how cinematic this game always was.

Metacritic 95/100

"A profoundly moving experience that proves games can tell stories like no other medium."

— Edge Magazine
#7 God of War

The reinvention of Kratos is one of gaming's greatest second acts. Trading Greek mythology for Norse, and pure rage for something far more complex — fatherhood. The single-shot camera technique alone is a technical marvel.

Metacritic 94/100

"A bold reinvention that somehow makes one of gaming's oldest icons feel completely fresh."

— Game Informer
#8 Minecraft

The best-selling game of all time for a reason. Minecraft is pure creative freedom — a blank canvas that has inspired an entire generation of gamers, developers and creators. Deceptively simple, endlessly deep.

Metacritic 93/100

"A generational game that has shaped how an entire generation thinks about creativity."

— Kotaku
#9 The Witcher 3

CD Projekt Red's open-world RPG set the gold standard for quest design. Every side quest tells a complete story. The world of the Continent feels genuinely alive. Still the benchmark for western RPGs nearly a decade on.

Metacritic 92/100

"The greatest RPG ever made, and possibly the greatest open-world game ever created."

— Gamespot
#10 Cyberpunk 2077

The greatest redemption arc in gaming history. Cyberpunk 2077 launched rough but has emerged as one of the most immersive RPGs ever made. Night City is an unparalleled achievement in world-building. Phantom Liberty sealed its legacy.

Metacritic 86/100

"Night City is the most realised game world ever created. A triumph of atmosphere and detail."

— VGC
